Is the Sunshine List still relevant for Ontarian tax payers?

 

Shaza Safi

Danielle Spring

Ben Mogil

Overview

The Sunshine List is annual list containing public sector employees in Ontario who have earned over $100,000 in the last year. The list is mandated by the Public Sector Salary Disclosure Act enacted by the Ontario government under Premier Mike Harris, with the first list released in 1996.

Objective

By analyzing public sector incomes for employees who have earned over $100,000 annually, we seek to determine if the yearly publication can be used to determine if the list is relevant for Ontarian taxpayers using a variety of criteria. For example, does disclosure equal transparency? Can the list be used to evaluate fairness in the Ontario workforce? In our analysis we use the Sunshine List data to seek out trends based on salaries, gender, industry, etc. The sunshine list does not denote gender therefore we will use a myraid of machine learning tools to predict gender.

We used employee wages by occupation annualy to compare public sector wages to private sector wages.

We used Consume Price Index (CPI) statistics to compare if consumer inflation over the years has been taken into account in the sunshine list wages.
